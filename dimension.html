Candy: It's alright, we get it

Phil: Anyway, it was an amazing shop. Little nooks and crannies everywhere. I have never seen so many really old books in one place

Mickey: Where is it?

Alana: Some lovely little shops in Bristol

Phil: Just give me a moment and I will explain

Alana: Ok, explain away

Terri: Doesn't sound like a great read

Phil: It wasn't

Candy: Why did you buy it then

Phil: I am getting to that

Jenny: In what way?

Terri: Ok

Phil: You know it has been said at times that the Pyramids may have been built by aliens?

Candy: I have heard that but I don't believe it

Micky: Nor do I.

Alana: Yes, like they would arrive, build a few Pyramids then fly off, seems very unlikely

Jenny: In what way?

Phil: Well, you know there are cave drawings of what look like space ships

Terri: I always thought that didn't make much sense

Terri: Considering that if they were aliens they would have had to come from thousands of light years away and the spaceships carved on the cave walls looked like the space equivalent of a Fiat Uno

Candy: But you just said you thought there was alien influence

Phil:I did, and I do think that?

Micky: So, where do you think they came from then? Mars?

Alana:That is very, very unlikely

Jenny: You got me confused now Phil. What exactly do you mean?

Terri: You mean a Fiat Uno (Laughter)

Candy: But then they would have to get through space to get those vehicles to Earth

Phil: Not necessarily

Alana: What do you mean

Phil: Not if they were on Earth already

Jenny: How much wine have you drunk Phil? Because you are not making any sense at all now

Phil: This is not as crazy as it might sound. Physicists work under the assumption that there are at least 10 dimensions, but the majority of us will never "see" them. Because we only know life in 3D, our brains don't understand how to look for anything more

Jenny: My brain has trouble just coping with this dimension.

Terri: Anyway Phil, what has this to do with the slip of paper you found?

Phil:I don't know who wrote it of course but it explains how to enter a different dimension

Candy: I am not sure I understand any of this

Phil: It appears not to make much sense I agree

Micky: Can you explain it better then?

Phil: I'll try

Alana: It's interesting but seems a but to weird to be true

Phil: I agree, it seems weird but it is believed that there, here on the Earth we know that there are, let's say, other worlds living alongside us

Jenny: I still don't get it

Phil: Ok, imagine a wafer biscuit

Terri: Right, I know what that is. Are you saying we live in a biscuit?

Phil: In a way yes! The biscuit is made up of several layers. Imagine the Earth is a bit like that. Several layers but in the normal course of life we only see and understand the layer we live on. The other layers will still be Earth but different people and different ways of life

Candy: I think I'll get my coat (laugh)

Micky: Just a minute. Phil may have something. I have actually read before about this. I believe even Einstein believed there was more than just the three dimensions we know

Phil: Exactly. He did but that is something a little different to what I am talking about

Alana: I am beginning to understand it now. You say you now have information on how to – well – kind of enter a different dimension to the one we know?

Phil: Yes! Exactly

Jenny: No, this is too unlikely. Are you saying Phil that you are the only person on Earth that has this information?

Phil: Who knows? There may be others but they just never talk about it

Terri: That seems unlikely

Phil: I agree, it does

Candy: So have you tried it Phil?

Phil: (a short pause) No, not yet

Mickey: Why not?

Phil: To be honest I didn't want to try it on my own

Alana: Why not?

Phil: I am not sure really, just felt a bit apprehensive about it and I did think that even if I did try it maybe people wouldn't believe that I had ever done it

Jenny: So this is the reason you invited us to dinner this evening?

Phil: Basically, yes. I wanted to ask you all if you would come with me

Terri: I don't mind doing it because in all honesty I think the slip of paper may be a hoax and absolutely nothing would happen

Phil: Unless we try it we would never know

Candy: Something we should bear in mind, If it isn't a hoax and we actually do pass into another dimension couldn't it be dangerous?

Phil: What do you mean?

Candy: You said that it would be like another world, with different people. If that is the case how do we know they would not be hostile?

Phil: That is a good point and probably why I have hesitated to try it

Mickey: I don't do dangerous, so this may not be for me

Phil: There is also something else that concerns me

Alana: What's that?

Phil: I think sometimes people may slip through by accident

Jenny: What makes you think that?

Phil: Sometimes people just disappear, right? Nobody ever sees them again and nobody knows where they went

Terri: Yes I have read about quite a few cases like that

Phil: And maybe you will laugh at this but what about the Bermuda Triangle?

Terri: You are thinking that the planes and ships that disappeared there slipped into a different dimension?

Phil: I'm saying it's possible

Candy: That is worrying then

Phil: Why?

Candy: Because all those planes and ships and the people inside them - never came back!

Mickey: Yes, Very good point

Phil: I did think about that, but there would be a difference

Alana: How?

Phil: Well, they disappeared by accident so would have had no idea how it happened or how to get back. We have instructions so it is very different

Jenny: No certainty though that you'd get back

Phil: I guess not but I would feel very confident about it

Terri: So are you going to try it?

Phil: Yes, if I can get someone to come with me

Candy: There is something else I am thinking

Phil: Ok

Candy: You described this as being like the layers of a wafer biscuit?

Phil: Kind of, yes

Candy: So if this is all possible and you slip through into another dimension, how do you know that you will come back to this one and not one of the other layers?

Mickey: Yes, that is a very good point Candy

Phil: I thought about that too, and think I have a plan to make sure we get back to this dimension.

Alana: You say we?

Phil: Well - yes. I was hoping you would all come with me

Jenny: I would. It sounds kind of exciting if it works

Terri: Hmm I'd think about it

Candy: I am pretty sure it isn't going to work anyway so I'd do it

Micky: I am not sure about this. If it does work it could be dangerous, we might not get back and perhaps it is something we should not mess with. Like you said, none of those people that disappeared ever came back

Alana: Oh come on Micky, don't be such a scaredy cat – you only live once

Micky: And if we do this, it may not be for much longer

Jenny: Yes come on Micky, it could be fun

Terri: When would we do it?

Phil: What about now? No time like the present

Candy: Should we take something with us? Like torches or a weapon or something

Phil: I don't have any weapons, or a torch

Micky: That is so typical. I left my kalishnikov at home this evening

Alana: That's better Micky, at least you can joke about it now

Jenny: So what exactly do we have to do Phil? Is there a secret place we have to go to or something like that?

Phil: No we can do it right here, with a bit of preparation

Toni: What sort of preparation

Phil: First I need a good plain wall

Candy: We could move the sofa a little and use that one

Phil: Good idea

(There is the sound of a little puffing and grunting as the sofa is moved)

Mickey: How's that?

Phil: Perfect. Now I need to copy some calculus from the note I found on to the wall

Alana: Won't that spoil the wall?

Phil: I can paint it again later

Jenny: Here, use this felt pen then

Phil: Ok, Can someone hold the note for me while I write

Toni: Here, I'll do it

Phil: Thanks Toni, Now here goes

Candy: Wow! That looks complicated

Mickey: I don't get this. The people in the Bermuda Triangle and the others that disappeared, I am assuming they didn't do this?

Phil: Quite right. But imagine the wafer biscuit has tiny holes in it. Some people and things have slipped through by accident but there is an actual gateway – which we are preparing now

Alana: I am still not convinced it will work

Phil: You may be right but I can't wait to try

Jenny: That is an awful lot of calculi you are writing up there Phil

Phil: And I am being very careful. It has to be perfect

Toni: Even if it doesn't work it looks very impressive

Candy: Looks like you are a science teacher

Mickey: How about the radiator?

Alana: Why do we need rope?

Phil: Candy raised a valid concern that we may not get back to the right layer, If we keep hold of the rope that could guide us back

Jenny: Are you sure about that?

Phil: No but it does kind of make sense. Are we all ready then? Lets all stand side by side and hold hands, we have to keep together. I'll grip the rope with my other hand

Toni: Ok, now what?

Phil: This sounds crazy I know, but just walk towards the wall, we should pass right through it

Candy: You are joking right?

Phil: No, Just walk slowly and hopefully it will work

Micky: This really does seem crazy

Alana: I agree but we have come this far so lets try

Jenny: Ok, let's walk

( A short pause)

Jenny: It is just light enough to see though
